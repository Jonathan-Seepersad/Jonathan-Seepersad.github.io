# Rally Rumble

Rally Rumble is a physics-based, local multi-player, demolision derby style game. Control RC cars while you ram into your opponent across 5 different levels and 8 unique cars. 6 different types of power-ups help to shake up

From a mid size team of 9, I played the role as a programmer. Most of my tasks revolved around getting the base controls and mechanics set up including:

- Driving controls to move physics based vehicles, using a state machine to map two anolog triggers among 5 different drive modes: driving, drifting, braking, reversing, and charging.
- Power-up system using a Gachapon to randomly decide effects between 6 different items: 
    - Silly String: Slows down opponent for a short time.
    - Funny Mirror: Reverses opponent's controlls for a short time.
    - Train: Summons a train that rams through, and deals heavy damage to, anything in it's pass.
    - Floor Cleaner: reduces traction for everyone, causing players to slip and slide.
    - Battery: Keeps charge meter activated, enabling you to attack your opponent in rapid succession.
    - Fuel Tank: Keeps charge meter activated *and* full, allowing you to do heavy damage.
- Ensure UI elements scale across multiple resolutions.

Aside from those main features in-game, I also managed the project's repository. Enforcing branch control, managing commits, doing code review and resolving conflicts.
